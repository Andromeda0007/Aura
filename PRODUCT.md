# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Primary: **teachers running a live class at an interactive smartboard** (large touchscreen, pen + touch), glancing at the screen from across a lit room while talking and writing. Secondary: **students**, who interact briefly via a shared screen or their own phones (taking a shared quiz, viewing a live board). The teacher's hands are busy and their attention is on the class — the UI must be operable at arm's length, voice-first, with almost no reading of small text.

## Product Purpose
Aura captures a live lesson (speech → text, whiteboard → OCR), fuses both into one running context, and on command generates grounded teaching content — quizzes, summaries, explanations, examples, diagrams, cleaned boards — plus dashboards, a content library, live student participation, and teacher time-savers (differentiation, lesson/worksheet generation, rubrics). Success = a teacher finishes class with materials already made and students already assessed, having touched the UI as little as possible.

## Brand Personality
**Confident · Calm · Alive.** Aura is a sharp, trustworthy teaching partner — never cutesy, never noisy. Voice is plain, encouraging, and precise (a great TA, not a hype bot). It should feel *crafted and premium* — the opposite of a generic, "vibe-coded" template. Energy comes from a few decisive moments (a result arriving, a quiz revealing), not from decoration everywhere.

## Anti-references
Explicitly NOT: generic AI-SaaS slop — gradient-text heroes, glassmorphism cards, endless identical icon-card grids, cream/sand "warm-neutral" body backgrounds, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows over every section, over-rounded everything, the big-number hero-metric template. NOT a toy/childish edtech look (rainbow, bubbly, Comic-Sans energy). NOT a dense enterprise dashboard. If someone could glance at it and say "an AI made that," it has failed.

## Design Principles
1. **Legible from the back of the room.** High contrast, large type and targets, no low-contrast gray-on-tint. The board and the active result are always the most prominent things.
2. **The board is the hero.** Teaching surface gets the space; chrome recedes. Voice-first; typing is the fallback.
3. **Calm canvas, decisive moments.** Quiet, spacious default; motion and color spent only where they carry meaning (a response landing, a correct answer, compression finishing).
4. **Instant, honest feedback.** Every action shows state — listening, processing, done, error — never a blank or a dead end.
5. **Earned trust.** Crafted detail, correct data, graceful failure. It should feel like a real product a school would adopt.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG **AA** minimum in BOTH light and dark themes (AAA for large headings), validated at projector/smartboard brightness and from a distance. Full keyboard navigation + visible focus; ARIA on controls. **Touch/pen targets ≥ 44–48px** (smartboard). `prefers-reduced-motion` honored on every animation. Captions/transcript double as an accessibility win. Light theme = bright lit-room default; dark = dimmed-room/glare reduction — both first-class.
